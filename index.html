Trendy Words in Twitter in the UK during the quarantine


Words and graphs by Huizhong Pang




The pandemic has become one of the popular topics for the past three months in the UK. According to the government policy for the Covid-19, most of the people start working at home, schools are forced to closed, as well as most shops, restaurants and pubs. Quarantine becomes everyone’s new lifestyle. Naturally, quarantine comes up as one of the Top Trending topics frequently on Twitter while it’s ongoing.

Content on Twitter reflects public living conditions. While people are hosting party at home, hashtag —— #LockdownHouseParty, ranks the fifth among all topics based on the data from Twitter. Analyzing contents on Twitter would help us to understand the circumstance of the public during the quarantine.

Top Words of #quarantine

Base on the tweets related to the hashtag —— #quarantine, let's take a look at the top words used in Tweets in May 2020 in the UK.


Within the #quarantine category, the word “quarantine” is the most frequent word, which is over four times more than the second most frequent word —— “Lockdown”. Except for the words directly related to “quarantine”, words like “stay home”, “art”, “free” and “love” occurred the top place in the word list.


All these tweets are posted by users on various mediums, including Twitter on different mediums like iPhone, Android, Web or other devices. Common words between different source could be different. We compared the top words from four different sources —— Twitter Web App, Twitter for Android, Twitter for iPhone and Instagram.


There is not much difference of the top there common words from these four sources. “Lockdown”, “quarantine life” and “coronavirus” are the most popular words within the hashtag (#quarantine). However, “music” and “love” rank higher on Instagram than other devices.

Sentiment Analysis of # quarantine

In order to understand the overall sentiment of text from #quarantine, natural language processing (NLP) was applied with these tweets.


From the sentiment analysis, the majority of the tweets with #quarantine contains positive emotion including joy, anticipation and trust. But fear is also a dominant emotion in the public.


According to the sentiment analysis by different sources, there is more positive mood including “joy” from Instagram than other devices. In general, most people hold an optimistic mood on Twitter during the quarantine in May 2020.

How about Boris Johnson’s tweets during the Coivd-19?

Boris Johnson, as the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, has been leading the country to fight against the coronavirus situation. His twitter account is more active during the quarantine, except for the period that he was tested positive to the Covid-19.


The next chart shows the top word of Boris Johnson’s tweets in 2020. “NHS”, which referred to the National Health Service in the UK, became the most common word within Boris’s tweets in 2020 so far. And most words in the list related to the pandemic.


The frequency of words changed throughout the period from January to May. For instance, the word “NHS” has only become common in Boris’s tweets since February.


Sentiment Analysis of Boris Johnson’s tweets

Based on the sentiment analysis of Boris Johnson’s Twitter account, the majority of his tweets express a positive tone through the words for the past five months.

Although most tweets from Boris Johnson shows positive, the proportion of emotions distribute differently each month.


This report shows that the pandemic has become the most concerned topic in the UK during this period. Luckily, a large scale of the public in the UK shows a positive attitude during the quarantine, including Boris Johnson.
